This HR Analytics Dashboard provides end-to-end visibility into workforce demographics, attrition patterns, employee satisfaction, and departmental performance. By integrating multiple HR data points, the report enables organizations to monitor employee health, identify retention risks, and support strategic workforce planning.

🔹 Key Workforce Highlights

Total Employees: 1,480

Average Tenure: 7.01 years

Attrition Count: 238

Attrition Rate: 16.08%

Gender Distribution: 889 Male | 591 Female

Employee base is dominated by the Sales department, followed by Research & Development, and Human Resources.

🔹 Attrition Insights

Highest attrition occurs among employees aged 26–35, followed by 18–25, indicating a retention challenge among younger talent.

Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives show the highest attrition among job roles.

Most exits occur in the < 5k salary range, highlighting compensation-related turnover.

Single employees contribute the highest share of attrition across marital status groups.

Life Sciences and Medical education backgrounds show the highest attrition counts.

Male employees show slightly higher attrition than female employees.

🔹 Departmental Performance & Employee Well-Being
Work-Life Balance

Highest work-life balance scores are seen in Sales Executives, Research Scientists, and Laboratory Technicians.

HR roles show comparatively lower work-life balance levels.

Department-Level WLB

Sales leads in overall work-life balance contribution, followed by R&D.

Human Resources contributes the least, indicating potential engagement or workload issues.

Average Tenure by Department

Sales, HR, and R&D all show strong average tenure levels, ranging from 7 to 8 years, indicating stable long-term employment across departments.

🔹 Workforce Distribution

Majority of employees hold degrees in Life Sciences, Medical, or Marketing.

Employee count is highest in the 26–35 and 36–45 age groups.

Education fields like Technical and HR represent a smaller portion of the workforce.

🔹 Manager Tenure & Retention

The highest attrition occurs among employees with 1–4 years under the same manager.

Very low attrition is observed among employees with 8+ years of manager tenure, showing strong long-term stability.

Managerial relationships play a significant role in retention patterns.

🎯 Purpose of the Dashboard

This dashboard helps stakeholders to:

Monitor workforce composition

Identify high-risk attrition segments

Assess equality in pay and gender distribution

Understand departmental workload and satisfaction

Make informed, data-driven HR decisions
